# DineCue Design System

## Visual Direction

DineCue is dark-first, premium, warm futuristic, cinematic, trustworthy, and food-aware. The interface should feel luxurious without becoming heavy or theatrical.

## Colors

- Base: deep graphite `#0D0D0F`, charcoal `#151412`, midnight `#171D22`.
- Text: warm ivory `#F4EFE4`, muted warm gray `#BDB3A3`.
- Accent: amber `#F0B35C`, copper `#D48A45`.
- Secondary accent: muted sage `#91A06B`.
- Caution/error: restrained wine `#6F363D`.

Avoid one-note palettes. Copper and amber should guide attention, not flood the page.

## Typography

Use expressive display typography for hero and major page titles. Use clean sans-serif text for product UI. Keep headings clear and hierarchy obvious, especially inside compact panels.

## Motion Principles

- Motion should feel purposeful and calm.
- Prefer opacity and transform animations.
- Use staggered reveals for recommendation cards.
- Use microinteractions on chips, buttons, and cards.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Avoid distracting loops except subtle plate/orbital motifs during generation.

## Component Principles

- Cards are glassy, warm, and restrained.
- Controls must be accessible, keyboard-friendly, and visibly focused.
- Buttons use icons when the action benefits from recognition.
- Empty states must look intentional and never fake backend data.
- Recommendation cards should be digestible and decision-oriented.
- Public product copy should feel human and concierge-like. Do not mention internal architecture terms in visible UI.
- Landing-page carousel examples are fictional presentation mocks only and should stay visually labeled as product preview content.

## Responsive Principles

Mobile is first-class. The app uses compact bottom navigation on mobile and a sidebar on desktop. Fixed-format elements need stable dimensions to avoid layout shifts.

## Dining Motif Rules

Use plate, table, glow, and orbital motifs tastefully. Do not scatter cheap food icons everywhere. The motif should imply service, warmth, and a prepared table.
